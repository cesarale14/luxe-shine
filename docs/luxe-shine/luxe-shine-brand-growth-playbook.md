# Luxe Shine — Brand & Growth Playbook

Market: Tampa, FL. Customer-facing brand: **Luxe Shine** (legal entity stays Luxe Shine Solutions LLC — never put "Solutions LLC" in a headline).

---

## 1. Brand Positioning Statement

**Internal (guides every decision):**
Luxe Shine is Tampa's operator-grade cleaning company for premium homes and short-term rentals. We don't sell cleaning — we sell never having to think about cleaning: photo-verified results, the same trained crew every visit, and turnovers that protect five-star reviews. Founded by an active STR operator who built the service he couldn't hire.

**Public one-liner:**
"Hotel-standard cleaning for Tampa homes and rentals."

**Positioning logic:**
- Every competitor claims "detail-focused" and "trustworthy." Adjectives are free; systems aren't. The premium position is built on **accountability infrastructure**: checklists, photo proof, SLAs, guarantees.
- Two proof pillars: (1) **Systems** — published checklist, photo verification before you're billed, response-time SLA. (2) **Skin in the game** — we run five Tampa STR listings ourselves; a missed clean costs us bookings too.
- Category framing: not a maid service. A **property care partner**. Price accordingly.

---

## 2. Ideal Customer Profiles

### Residential — "The Delegator"
- Dual-income professionals, execs, physicians, attorneys. 30–55. Homes $500K+ in South Tampa, Hyde Park, Davis Islands, Harbour Island, Palma Ceia, Westchase.
- Values time over money. Wants a recurring schedule (biweekly default) and zero mental load.
- Pain: last cleaner was inconsistent — late, quality drifted, had to re-explain preferences every visit.
- Buying triggers: move-in, new baby, hosting season, just fired the previous cleaner.
- Core objection: "Are you insured? Can I trust you alone in my home?"
- Why they matter: recurring biweekly clients are the cash-flow backbone of the whole business.

### STR — two sub-profiles
**(a) Self-managing host, 1–5 units**
- Concentrated in Channelside/Downtown (33602), Hyde Park (33606), Seminole Heights.
- Coordinates turnovers over text. Pain: no-shows, same-day turns, guest complaints about cleanliness (the #1 review killer), restocking chaos.
- KPI they buy: **zero missed check-ins**.

**(b) Small property manager / co-host, 10–50 units**
- Needs a vendor that scales: flat rate per unit type, photo confirmation before every check-in, damage reporting, one monthly invoice.
- Buying trigger: their solo cleaner ghosted on a Saturday, or they just signed 8 new doors.

## 3. Service Menu (four services, keep it this tight)

| Service | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Signature Clean** | Recurring weekly/biweekly maintenance. The core residential product. |
| **The Deep Reset** | Deep clean — also the **mandatory onboarding clean** for every new recurring client. |
| **Move-Ready Clean** | Move-in/move-out, vacant-home spec. Realtor/PM referral product. |
| **Five-Star Turnover** | STR turnover: clean + stage + restock + timestamped photo report + damage/inventory flags. |

**Add-ons:** inside fridge/oven, interior windows, laundry & linen service, garage/patio, consumable restock program (STR).

**Rules that protect the premium position:**
- Every new residential client starts with a Deep Reset. It resets the baseline, protects recurring quality, and prices the onboarding correctly.
- Flat-rate STR pricing by unit type (studio / 1BR / 2BR / 3BR+). Same-day turnover carries a surcharge — urgency is a feature, price it.
- No hourly rates published anywhere. Quotes only. Set a visit minimum and hold it.

## 5. Website Messaging Strategy (build spec)

**Page map (5 nav items max):** Home · Residential · STR Turnovers · Our Standard · Request a Quote

**Home page section order:**
1. **Hero** — headline options (pick one):
   - "Hotel-standard cleaning for Tampa homes and rentals."
   - "The cleaning company you never have to think about."
   - "Five-star clean. Every home. Every turnover."
   Subhead: "Insured, background-checked crews. Photo-verified results. Trusted by Tampa homeowners and Airbnb hosts."
   Primary CTA: **Request a Quote**. Secondary CTA: **STR Turnover Quote**.
2. **Trust strip** — Insured & bonded · Background-checked · Photo-verified · 24-hr guarantee.
3. **Dual-path selector** — "For Your Home" / "For Your Rental." Segments visitors in one click.
4. **How it works** — Quote within 2 business hours → Deep Reset onboarding → Your standing schedule.
5. **The Standard** — publish the actual cleaning checklist. Competitors hide theirs; transparency here is a differentiator nobody can copy without doing the work.
6. **Proof** — Google reviews, before/after gallery, photo-verification explainer.
7. **STR block** — the "we host too" story (see below).
8. **Service area** — name the neighborhoods. Never "Tampa Bay area."
9. **FAQ** — insurance, supplies, pets, key/access handling, rescheduling, guarantee.
10. **Final CTA.**

**STR Turnovers page:**
- Lead with the cost of a bad turnover: one missed clean = a canceled booking plus a review that drags the listing for months.
- Then the system: photo confirmation before every check-in, restock report, damage flags with photos, same-day capability, flat rate per unit type.
- Proof block: *"We operate five Tampa STR listings ourselves. Five-Star Turnover is the system we built to protect our own reviews."*
- CTA: Request a Turnover Quote (unit count, platform, turnover frequency, zip).

**Quote form:** short. Name, phone, email, zip, home size or unit count, service type, preferred date. State the SLA on the form: "Quotes within 2 business hours."

**Voice rules:**
- Specific beats superlative. Numbers beat adjectives.
- Banned: "we treat your home like our own," exclamation points, stock-photo energy, "affordable."
- Write like a confident operator, not a brochure.

## 6. Trust-Building Angles

- Insurance + bonding stated on-site, certificate available on request.
- Background-checked, **consistent** crews — same cleaner(s) every recurring visit, stated as policy.
- Photo verification after every job, sent to the client. This is the single strongest trust mechanic — it converts skeptics and it's your review-ask trigger.
- The Luxe Shine Guarantee: anything missed, re-cleaned within 24 hours, free.
- Published checklist (radical transparency).
- Owner accountability: clients get a direct line, not a call center.
- "We host too" — skin-in-the-game story for the STR segment.
- Local specificity: named neighborhoods, local landmarks in photos.
- Visual professionalism: branded shirts, shoe covers, labeled supply kit. Premium clients notice the details before you say a word.

## 7. 90-Day Growth Plan

### Days 1–30 — Foundation
**Goal: site live, review engine on, 10 Google reviews.**
- Google Business Profile fully built: correct categories, all services listed, photos uploaded weekly, Q&A seeded.
- Website live with quote flow and the 2-hour SLA.
- Shoot the entire photo library at your own STR units + one or two willing client homes (shot list in §8).
- Write both checklists (residential + STR) as real documents.
- Personally text every past and current client the direct Google review link this week.
- Lock pricing floors, unit-type flat rates, and the Deep Reset onboarding rule.
- Insurance/bonding confirmed, certificate PDF ready to send.

### Days 31–60 — STR wedge
**Goal: 5 STR units under contract, 20 total reviews.**
- Direct outreach: Tampa host Facebook groups, co-host networks, local STR meetups/REIA. Identify self-managed listings in 33602/33606/Seminole Heights and reach out directly.
- Trial offer: a paid **audit turnover** at an intro rate — low-friction way to demo the photo-report system. Not free; premium brands don't work free.
- Partnerships: 5 realtors (Move-Ready referrals), 2–3 stagers and property managers.
- Residential channel: Nextdoor presence; "we clean for your neighbor" door cards in the target neighborhood after every job; Google Local Services Ads once reviews ≥ 15.
- Weekly scorecard goes live (below).

### Days 61–90 — Systemize + compound
**Goal: 25+ reviews, 8–12 recurring residential clients, 6–10 STR units on schedule, first hire trained.**
- Turn your checklists into SOPs. Train a lead cleaner. Your job shifts to inspection and quoting, not cleaning.
- Referral program: account credit per referred recurring client; discounted turnover per referred host.
- If close rate exceeds ~60%, raise quoted prices for new clients — that's price discovery telling you you're underpriced.
- Write one STR case study with numbers: units served, turnovers completed, missed check-ins (zero), host review score held.
- Decision point: scale LSA budget vs. double down on host outreach, based on cost per acquired client.

### Weekly scorecard (track from day 31)
Leads in · Quotes sent · Close rate · Recurring clients (total) · Turnovers/week · Google reviews (total) · Missed/late jobs (must stay 0)

## 8. Photos & Assets to Collect

**Photography (your own STR units are the portfolio — no permission needed, already staged):**
- Before/after pairs: same angle, same light, 10–15 pairs. Kitchens, bathrooms, bedrooms.
- "Hotel reveal" finished shots: crisp bed corners, folded towel stacks, coffee tray, staged counters. These are the money shots for premium perception.
- Detail macros: polished faucet, clean grout line, oven interior.
- Owner/team: branded shirt, shoe covers, supply kit laid out, a straight headshot.
- Process shots: the photo-verification moment, checklist on a tablet.
- Vertical video: 15–30s satisfying-clean clips and a turnover time-lapse. This content category performs organically on Reels/TikTok and costs nothing.
- Technique: natural light, horizontal for web + vertical for social, declutter every frame, consistent editing.

**Non-photo assets:**
- Checklist PDFs (residential + STR), insurance certificate, guarantee one-pager, quote template, and a review-link QR card left behind after every clean.

## 9. Proof to Build as a Small Company

1. **Google reviews** — the single most valuable local asset. Target 25 in 90 days. System: ask at the moment of delight (right after the photo report lands), text the direct link, one follow-up. Respond to every review.
2. **Photo-verified job counter** — start counting rooms/jobs now; "X rooms photo-verified" compounds forever.
3. **Named written guarantee** — on the site, on the quote, on the invoice.
4. **A response-time stat you actually track** — "average quote time: under 2 hours."
5. **One STR case study** with real numbers by day 90.
6. **Your own hosting record** — if your listing ratings are strong, cite them (verify the exact numbers before publishing anything).
7. **The boring proof** — LLC, EIN, insurance cert. Premium clients quietly check.
8. **Consistency as policy** — "same crew every visit" stated publicly.

## 10. Mistakes to Avoid

- **Competing on price.** One matched cheap quote poisons the positioning and fills capacity with bad-fit clients. Let the cheap leads go.
- **Taking every client.** Wrong-fit clients at low prices block premium capacity. Reprice or release them early.
- **Over-wide service area.** Drive time is invisible margin death. Tight radius until you're dense, then expand.
- **One missed or late job in the first 90 days.** Reliability is the entire product. Buffer the schedule; never book what you can't guarantee.
- **Publishing hourly rates.** Hourly framing makes you a commodity comparison.
- **A template site with stock photos.** Premium clients smell it in two seconds. Real photos of real work only.
- **Skipping the Deep Reset onboarding.** Recurring quality drifts from a bad baseline, then the client churns "mysteriously."
- **Underpricing same-day STR turns.** Same-day capability is your most valuable STR feature — it carries a surcharge.
- **Hiring before SOPs.** Scaling chaos just makes bigger chaos.
- **Paid ads before the GBP + reviews foundation.** You'll pay for clicks that land on zero proof and don't convert.
- **The single-operator bottleneck.** If every clean routes through you personally, capacity caps hard and reliability breaks the first week you're pulled onto anything else. First hire is a lead cleaner plus an inspection system — earlier than feels comfortable.

## Strategic Note — The Flywheel

Your STR cleaning clients are hosts and small PMs — the exact profile that buys STR software. The turnover-coordination and photo-verification pain Luxe Shine solves manually today is product surface area later. Build the service brand clean and standalone, but log every operational pain point clients describe; that list is a roadmap.
